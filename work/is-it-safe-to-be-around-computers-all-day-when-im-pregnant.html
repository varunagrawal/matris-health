is it safe to be around computers all day when I'm pregnant?

A lot of research has been carried out in the West on whether sitting in front of a computer all day causes birth defects or miscarriage -- and the studies indicate that there's no evidence that it does.

Of course, sitting anywhere for hours on end won't do you any good, so get up and walk around every few hours.

If your job involves sitting long hours at the computer, ensure that your posture is correct and doesnâ€™t strain your back. As far as possible, choose a chair which is straight backed and gives adequate support. Try using a cushion or foam wedge to support the small of your back. Keeping a low stool to elevate your feet is also a good idea as is changing your position frequently. Set a timer or an alarm as a reminder to stretch your legs every 30 minutes or so. This also relieves eye strain caused by constantly focusing on a screen.

If using a laptop, always keep it on a worktable at a convenient height and not on your lap since it radiates a lot of heat. Connect an external mouse to your laptop if it has a toggle or touch pad since these options may strain the wrist muscles and tendons. Likewise the keyboard of a computer should be ergonomically placed so as to minimise strain.
