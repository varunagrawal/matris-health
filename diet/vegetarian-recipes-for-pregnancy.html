With a little bit of planning, a vegetarian diet can provide all the nutrients you need for a healthy pregnancy.

Your body requires a little more protein during pregnancy, and this is easily provided by a healthy varied vegetarian diet. Take extra care to ensure you have an adequate intake of two important nutrients, namely iron and calcium. Here are some tasty vegetarian recipes to inspire you.

Sprouts, corn and vegetable salad

Ingredients:
  • ¼ cup moong sprouts
  • ¼ cup chana sprouts or (kala chana)
  • ¼ cup corn (makai) (steamed)
  • 1 carrot (gajar)
  • 1 cucumber (kheera)
  • 1 small broccoli (hari gobhi)
  • Few lettuce (salad patta) leaves
  • 1 tsp green chilli paste
  • 2 tsps green coriander (dhania) paste
  • 1 tsp oil
  • ½ tsp cumin seeds (jeera)
  • ½ tsp mustard seeds (rai or sarson)
  • Pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • 2 tsps lemon juice
  • Few curry leaves (kadhi patta)
  • ½ tsp chat masala
  • Coriander (dhania) leaves
  • Salt to taste
Method:
Chop all the vegetables finely and set aside.
Heat oil in a pan and add mustard seeds, cumin seeds and asafoetida. Add curry leaves and chana sprouts. Cover with a lid and cook for 2 minutes.

Now add moong sprouts and corn. Add a little salt and fry for a few minutes. Cover with the lid again and allow to cool for 15 to 20 minutes.

Mix the ingredients with the chopped vegetables in a salad bowl. Add chat masala, lemon juice and a little salt. Mix well, garnish with coriander and serve.

Split urad dal with spinach (paalak)

A simple dish which makes any meal complete, the split urad dal with the skin adds fibre and protein. Calcium, vitamins and iron are the other key nutrients.

Ingredients:
  • ½ cup split urad dal with the skin
  • 2 cups spinach (paalak), chopped and cleaned
  • 2 cloves garlic (lehsun) minced
  • 1 inch piece of ginger, chopped fine
  • 1 onion
  • 1 green chilli, slit
  • 1 tomato, chopped
  • ½ tsp turmeric powder (haldi)
Tempering (tadka):
  • 1 tsp oil
  • ½ tsp red chilli powder
  • 1 tsp cumin (jeera) seeds
  • Pinch of asafoetida (hing)
Method:
Wash the dal well and put into a cooker.

Add 3 cups of water, turmeric powder, chopped ginger, garlic, slit green chilli and the chopped tomato. Cook for 10 minutes (3 whistles), remove when cool and mash well together.

Take a heavy pan or karahi and heat the oil for tempering. Add the cumin seeds, when they start to crackle, add the hing and red chilli powder. After half a minute, add the cooked dal, salt and the chopped spinach.

Add water if necessary and cook for ten minutes till the spinach is well cooked and blended with the dal. Serve with rotis or rice.

Hearty beans and vegetable soup

Hearty enough to make a meal by itself, this tasty soup is a medley of protein rich beans, vegetables rich in fiber, vitamin, folate and iron.

Ingredients:
  • 1 cup mixed beans like kidney beans (rajma), cow peas (lobia) and chickpeas (chana), soaked overnight and pressure cooked for two whistles
  • 1 carrot (gajar),grated
  • 1 beetroot (chukandar), diced
  • 200gms pumpkin (kaddu), diced
  • Half zucchini or (torai), diced
  • 2 onions, chopped
  • 6 tomatoes, puréed
  • 1 red capsicum (lal shimla mirch) diced
  • 1 green capsicum (shimla mirch), diced
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 3 cloves garlic (lehsun)
  • 1 tsp chilli (lal mirch) powder
  • 1 tsp coriander (dhania) powder
  • 1 tsp cumin (jeera) powder
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • ½ cup chopped mint (pudina) leaves
  • 6 cups vegetable stock or 1 stock cube dissolved in half a litre of water
  • Salt to taste
  • Freshly crushed black peppercorns (sabut kali mirch)
Method:
Heat the oil in a large heavy pan. Add the onions, carrot, beetroot, peppers, and garlic. Sauté for about 15 minutes or until the onions are translucent. Add the chili powder, coriander, cumin and oregano cook for 2 minutes.

Stir in the tomatoes purée and salt. If you are using a stock cube add less salt. Add the vegetable stock, pumpkin, zucchini and mixed beans.

Bring to a boil and then simmer over medium flame until the vegetables are tender, about 20-25 minutes. Add the crushed pepper and salt. Serve garnished with mint leaves.

Crispy lotus stem (kamal kakri)

Easy to make and delicious to eat. It is rich in fibres and contains good amounts of vitamin A, vitamin C and iron.

Serves two
Ingredients:
  • 300 gms lotus stem (kamal kakri), washed and cleaned
  • 1 tsp chilli (lal mirch) powder
  • 1 tsp cumin (jeera) powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tbsp oil
Method:
Slice the lotus stem into circles of medium thickness (should not be too thin or too thick).

Boil the slices in salted water for about 5-8 minutes till half cooked. Cool and pat dry with a clean towel to absorb all the moisture.

Pre heat the oven to about 200 degrees C. In an oven-proof tray toss the slices in a mixture of chilli powder, cumin powder and salt and drizzle oil over them.

Bake for about 10-15 minutes turning over half way between till they are crisp on the outside and golden brown while a bit chewy towards the centre.

Vegetable and dal idli

This tasty dish is an excellent source of folic acid, iron and vitamin C.
Ingredients:
  • ½ cup split bengal gram (chana dal)
  • ½ cup split pigeon pea (arhar or toor dal)
  • ¼ cup split yellow gram (peeli moong dal)
  • ¼ cup green peas (matar), boiled and mashed
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 1 small carrot (gajar), grated
  • 1 cup fenugreek (methi) leaves, chopped
  • 2 cups chopped coriander (dhania)
  • ¼ cup coconut, grated
  • 3 green chillis, chopped
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tsp oil for greasing
Method:
Thoroughly wash all the dals and soak them together for atleast three hours.

Drain and grind to a smooth paste, by adding a little water. Add the green chillies, coriander, coconut, green peas, fenugreek onion, carrot and salt. Add water as required to make a thick batter. Grease idli moulds and pour the batter into them. Steam for 10 to 12 minutes till done. Eat with coconut chutney.

Dal parantha

Wheat flour and chana dal are both rich in protein and this parantha makes for a very satisfying and nutritious meal.

Ingredients:
  • ½ cup chana (dal), soaked for at least 2 hours
  • 1 cup wheat flour (gehun ka atta)
  • 1 tsp carom (ajwain) seeds
  • ½ tsp chilli powder
  • ½ tsp cumin (jeera) powder
  • Pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • ½ cup chopped coriander (dhania) leaves
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tbsp oil or clarified butter (ghee)
  • ½ tsp turmeric (haldi) powder
Method:
Cook the chana dal in a pressure cooker with very little water till soft and dry.

Knead the wheat flour with water, one tsp oil and salt to smooth dough. Let it rest.
In a pan, heat the oil and add the ajwain, and hing. When it changes colour, add the chilli powder, turmeric powder and dal. Sauté for about 3-4 minutes till dry.

Add coriander leaves and let cool. Divide the dough into equal balls. Take a ball of dough and roll out, take a smaller ball of the cooked dal filling and place it in the centre of the rolled out chapati.

Gather all sides of the dough and close over the dal filling. Dip in loose flour and roll out again gently into a parantha. Roast the parantha on a hot griddle (tava) till there are faint brown spots on both side. Add ½ tsp of oil or ghee and cook till it golden brown. Repeat with the rest of the dough and filling. Serve plain, with curd or with a vegetable of your choice.

Note: You can substitute chana dal with moong dal for a variation. You can also use leftover cooked dal to knead the flour for delicious dal paranthas.

Aviyal

This nutritious curry is a good source of potassium, vitamin A, vitamin C, anti-oxidants, iron, calcium, fibre and zinc.

Ingredients:
  • 200 gm ash gourd (petha)
  • 100gm Indian yam (arbi)
  • 1 medium sized carrot (gajar)
  • 1 medium sized raw banana
  • 6 to 8 French beans (frans been)
  • 1 drumstick (sejanki ki phalli)
  • 6-8 broad beans (sem ki phalli)
  • ¼ cup green peas (matar)
  • ½ cup grated coconut
  • 1 ½ cups curd/yoghurt
  • 10-12 curry leaves (kadhi patta)
  • 2 to 3 green chilli (hari mirch)
  • ½ tsp cumin (jeera) seeds
  • 1 tbsp rice, soaked for an hour
  • Salt to taste
  • ½ tsp turmeric (haldi) powder
  • 1 to 2 tbsp coconut oil
Method:
Cut the ash gourd, yams, carrots, banana, beans and drumsticks into 2 inch long baton shaped pieces. Keep aside.

Mix the coconut, green chillies, cumin seeds and rice to a fine paste. Whisk this mixture with curd and keep aside.

Boil arbi separately in salted water and drain it. Boil carrot, ash gourd, raw banana, French beans, broad beans, drumstick, peas and curry leaves in one and half cups of salted water. When vegetables are almost done, add the curd mixture and boiled arbi and stir thoroughly.

Bring to simmering point and remove from gas. Garnish with coconut oil.

Aloo poha

This traditional dish is an excellent source of iron.

Ingredients:
  • 2 cups beaten flakes (poha, chivda)
  • ½ cup potato, peeled and cubed
  • ½ cup onions, chopped
  • ½ tsp mustard (rai/ sarson) seeds
  • 6 to 8 curry leaves (kadhi patta)
  • 2 to 3 green chilli, slit
  • ½ tsp cumin (jeera) seeds
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • 1 tsp sugar (optional)
  • 2 tbsp coriander (dhania), chopped
  • 4 lemon wedges
  • ½ tsp turmeric (haldi) powder
Method:
Put the poha in a sieve and hold it under running water for a 10 to 15 seconds.

Drain out all excess water and keep aside. Heat the oil in a non-stick pan and add the mustard seeds and cumin seeds. As the seeds crackle, add the green chillies and curry leaves. Stir for one minute.

Add the onions and sauté till the onions turn golden brown. Then add the potato, haldi, salt and a little water. Cover and heat on medium flame till the potatoes are cooked. Add the poha, sugar and a little more salt and mix well. Garnish with the coriander and lemon wedges. Serve hot.

Note: In case the poha gets too dry, you can add a tbsp. of milk to make it moist. Mix well and serve immediately.

Dalia pulao

This healthy pulao is packed with fibre, vitamin, vitamin A, magnesium, iron.

Ingredients:
  • 1 ½ cups broken wheat (dalia) or oats
  • 10 to 12 leaves spinach (paalak), chopped
  • 2 small carrots (gajar), chopped to ½ inch pieces
  • 4 to 5 French beans (frans been),
  • 1 medium sized onion, sliced
  • ¼ cup green peas (matar)
  • 3to 4 cloves (laung)
  • 7 to 8 black pepper corns ( sabut kali mirch)
  • 1 bay leaf (tej patta)
  • 1 inch stick cinnamon (dalchini)
  • 2 to 3 green chilli, slit
  • 1 tsp cumin (jeera) seeds
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tbsp oil
Method:
Heat the oil in a pan. Add bay leaf, cumin seeds, peppercorns, cinnamon and cloves.
Sauté till they begin to change colour. Add the sliced onion and sauté till they turn translucent.

Add carrots and sauté. Add French beans and spinach leaves. Add dalia. Mix well and serve warm.

Radish greens (mooli saag)

Radish and radish leaves are great source of vitamin C, calcium potassium, folic acid and copper.

Ingredients:
  • 4 medium radishes (mooli) with leaves
  • ½ tsp mustard (rai/sarson) seeds
  • ½ tsp cumin (jeera) seeds
  • ½ tsp turmeric (haldi) powder
  • 1 tsp red chilli powder
  • 1 tsp dry mango (amchur) powder
  • 1 ½ tsp sugar
  • Pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tbsps oil
Method:
Wash the radish and the leaves well. Chop the white part of the radish into small cubes and shred the leaves. Sprinkle a little salt on the radishes and set aside for twenty minutes. Drain off the liquid.

Heat oil in a karahi and add mustard seeds. When they crackle add cumin seeds and hing and sauté for half a minute. Add red chilli powder and haldi. Sauté for ten seconds. Add radish and leaves. Cook for a minute.

Sprinkle a little water to prevent scorching. Cover and cook on medium heat for ten minutes or till the radish is soft and done. Add salt to your taste. Sprinkle sugar and dry mango powder and mix well. Serve hot with chapatis or paranthas.

Soya and coconut curry

This dish is rich in protein, fibre and phosphorous.
Ingredients:
  • ½ cup coconut (nariyal), grated
  • 2 cups soya nuggets, soaked in hot water for 5 minutes
  • 2 large onions, chopped
  • 2 large tomatoes (tamatar), chopped
  • 1 tsp mustard (rai/sarson) seeds
  • ½ tsp aniseed (saunf) seeds
  • ½ tsp turmeric (haldi) powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala powder
  • 5 to 6 curry leaves (kadhi patta)
  • 1 tbsp coriander (dhania) leaves, chopped
  • Salt to taste
  • ¼ cup oil
Drain and blend the soaked soya nuggets to a smooth paste.

Heat the oil in pan. Fry mustard, aniseed and onion till light brown. Add chopped tomatoes, turmeric powder, salt and garam masala. When the oil separates add the soya paste. Add coconut and little water and bring to boil.

Garnish with coriander leaves and curry leaves. Eat warm with rotis, naans or even with some brown bread.
