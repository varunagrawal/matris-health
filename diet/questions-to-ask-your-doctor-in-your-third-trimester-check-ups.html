questions to ask your doctor in your third trimester check-ups

You have reached the final phase of your pregnancy, the third trimester. You may find your excitement growing as you get closer to seeing your baby but also you might be a bit scared about labour and birth. Such mixed emotions are absolutely normal.

You are most likely to have a visit scheduled with your doctor every two weeks from 28 to 36 weeks of pregnancy, then switch to once-a-week visits until you have your baby.

Asking questions during your checkups is important to help you understand what to expect and to help you understand what you and your baby are going through. Here is a list of possible questions for the third trimester to act as a memory jogger. You won't want to ask all of them. It's unlikely you will have time as most doctors are busy. Read through the ones that interest you and then decide if there are more questions you want to ask your doctor.

  • What kind of tests can I expect now and why are they done?
  • When should I start my maternity leave?
  • Is there any change in my vitamin supplements?
  • Can I continue to exercise, clean the house and climb stairs?
  • What is the recommended weight gain for this period?
  • Is it normal for my feet to swell up? What can I do to bring the swelling down?
  • Is it safe to have sex during the third trimester?
  • I have abdominal pain, should I be concerned?
  • What do contractions feel like? Are they the only signs of labour?
  • I can't feel my baby kicking or moving? Is she all right?
  • What is preterm labour?
  • What should I do if my waters break or if I go into labour in the middle of the night?
  • If I choose, can I have an epidural?
  • If my baby is in the breech position do I have to have a a c-section?
  • Can my birth partner be in the delivery room?
  • How do vaginal deliveries usually happen?
  • What procedures will I go through in labour?
  • What happens just after the birth?
  • How will I know how to breastfeed?
