questions to ask your doctor in your second trimester check-ups

The middle three months of pregnancy are known as your 'second trimester'. By now, you and your doctor have probably established a good rapport and you will have had at least 3 visits at different intervals.

Asking questions during your checkups is important to help you understand what to expect and to understand what is happening to you and your baby. No question is stupid so ask as many as time permits. Here is a list of possible questions for the second trimester to act as a memory jogger. You won't want to ask all of them. It's unlikely you will have time as most doctors are busy. Read through the ones that interest you and then decide if there are more questions you want to ask your doctor.

  • What tests do I need to take during this time?
  • Will I have another ultrasound?
  • What do I change in my eating habits at this stage?
  • Can I take any medication for my backaches, heartburn or leg cramps?
  • Do I need to go on taking antenatal vitamins?
  • How much weight should I gain during these three months?
  • Is it safe to travel by road, sea or air during this period?
  • What kind of exercise is safe for this trimester?
  • Can I continue my exercise workouts?
  • Can I continue to work?
  • Is it normal for my skin to darken?
  • What situation(s) should I consider as an emergency and call my doctor?
  • Is it safe to have sex in the second trimester?
  • Should I sleep with a pillow under my belly?
